\chapter{The Mellin Transform}
  Throughout, \(s = \s+it\) and \(u = \tau+ir\) will stand for complex variables with \(\s\), \(\tau\), \(t\), and \(r\) real.

  Let \(f(x)\) be a function on \((0,\infty)\) such that \(f(x) = O(x^{-a})\) as \(x \to 0\) and \(f(x) = O(x^{-b})\) as \(x \to \infty\) for some real \(a\) and \(b\) with \(a < b\). Its \textbf{Mellin transform}\index{Mellin transform} \((\mc{M}f)(s)\) is defined by
  \[
    (\mc{M}f)(s) = \int_{0}^{\infty}f(x)x^{s-1}\,dx.
  \]
  for \(a < \s < b\). It is absolutely convergent precisely because of the asymptotics satisfies by \(f(x)\) upon writing
  \[
    (\mc{M}f)(s) = \int_{0}^{1}f(x)x^{s-1}\,dx+\int_{1}^{\infty}f(x)x^{s-1}\,dx.
  \]
